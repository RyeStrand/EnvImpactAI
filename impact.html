IMPACT

Social and Ethical Impacts

The rise of generative AI has profound social and ethical implications. Issues such as bias in AI models, the potential for misinformation, and the displacement of jobs due to automation are critical concerns. Additionally, the use of AI in surveillance and decision-making raises questions about privacy, accountability, and fairness.
It is essential to ensure that AI technologies are developed and deployed responsibly, with a focus on inclusivity and equity. Addressing these challenges requires collaboration between policymakers, technologists, and society at large.

Environmental & Climate Impact

The environmental impact of AI is significant, particularly due to the energy-intensive processes involved in training large models. Data centers that power AI systems consume vast amounts of electricity, often relying on non-renewable energy sources. This contributes to greenhouse gas emissions and exacerbates climate change.
Efforts to mitigate these impacts include optimizing AI algorithms for energy efficiency, transitioning to renewable energy sources, and promoting sustainable practices in the tech industry. These steps are crucial to reducing the carbon footprint of AI technologies.
